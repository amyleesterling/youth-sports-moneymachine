## Methodology

Nationally representative sample of 1,032 youth-sport parents (plus 13 community samples), hosted by
Qualtrics. Questions concern the parent's OLDEST child active in sport (mean age 12.50, range 6–18).
"Regular" participation = ≥35 practice/training/competition days in the prior 12 months.
Mean household pre-tax income $90,907.79 (the source report is internally inconsistent on this mean,
also printing $119,267.31; both reproduced as printed). All 50 states represented.

## Spending differences by child gender (inferential)

Parents were significantly MORE likely to cross every spending-share-of-income threshold for
daughters than sons (>1% of income: 28.9% vs 23.0%; >2%: 16.7% vs 12.2%; >5%: 6.9% vs 4.1%;
>10%: 2.8% vs 1.8%; all significant). Category models: travel/lodging (F single-sport $404 vs
M $155, p=.002) and lessons/instruction (F single-sport $282 vs M $164, p=.005) drove the gap.

No significant spending differences by community type (urban/suburban/rural).
