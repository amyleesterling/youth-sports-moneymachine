# 2024 National Youth Sports Parent Survey — cost-relevant tables (primary source excerpt)

**Source:** The Aspen Institute Project Play; analysis by Dr. Travis E. Dorsch (Utah State
University, Families in Sport Lab) and Dr. Jordan A. Blazo (Louisiana Tech University).
Original file: `National-Youth-Sports-Parent-Survey-FINAL.pdf` (148 pp)
(https://www.aspeninstitute.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/National-Youth-Sports-Parent-Survey-FINAL.pdf).

> Machine-readable excerpt verified against the original PDF. Reproduces the tables this site's
> data derives from. The full report also contains group-difference analyses by community type,
> race/ethnicity, child sex, household income, child age, state, and region, omitted here for brevity.

## Methodology

Nationally representative online survey of **1,848 youth-sports parents** (children 6–18,
participating regularly in one or more sports), all 50 states + DC, fielded **Nov–Dec 2024**.
Spending questions cover the child's PRIMARY sport and, separately, the same child's OTHER sports.

primary-sport table from the report itself; this site's category comparison uses it against the

## Why no 2024 figure exists for hockey, golf, swim, tennis, lacrosse, volleyball, gymnastics, or cheer

The per-sport breakout covers only the six most-common primary sports. PRIMARY-sport
respondent counts for the others were too small to publish means (pp. 16–17):

| Sport | Primary-sport n (of 1,848) | Participants n |
|---|---|---|
| Ice hockey | 16 | 41 |
| Golf | 15 | 88 |
| Lacrosse | 13 | 32 |
| Tennis | 31 | 101 |
| Swimming | 34 | 133 |
| Gymnastics | 54 | 137 |
| Volleyball (court or sitting) | 58 | 105 |
| Cheerleading | 58 | 126 |

This confirms, at the primary source, that the widely-recycled "$2,583 hockey" figure has no
2024 counterpart — it is 2019 data — and that competitive cheer has never had a published
national spending figure in any survey wave (2019: 21 respondents under "Other sports";
2024: 58 primary-sport respondents, not broken out).

## Other sports spending (same child, non-primary, p. 114)

Six-sport totals: basketball $605.98, soccer $412.19, baseball $522.64, tackle football $397.75,
flag football $303.20, dance $539.31; overall other-sports total $528.71 (no statistically
significant differences by primary sport). Combined with the primary-sport mean, average
per-child spending ≈ $1,500/yr.
