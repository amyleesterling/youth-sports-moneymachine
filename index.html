The youth sports money machine

Everyone "knows" travel sports costs are exploding, and the favorite villain is private equity. This page tests that story against the actual price data, sport by sport — in constant dollars, with the acquisition dates drawn on the charts so you can judge for yourself.

All figures independently sourced; every number is footnoted. Where the data doesn't exist, we say so instead of interpolating.

Typical annual all-in for travel-sports families; 21% top $5,000/yr [src]

Why the averages look low: only about 17% of kids play travel/club as their primary setting [src] — the survey average blends a $50 town-league season with a $12,000 AAA hockey year. The averages are the only numbers with a real trend line, so they lead the trend chart below; the club-level fee dataset after it shows what the travel tier actually costs.
