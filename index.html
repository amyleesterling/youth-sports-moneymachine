The youth sports money machine

Everyone "knows" travel sports costs are exploding, and the favorite villain is private equity. This page tests that story against the actual price data, sport by sport — in constant dollars, with the acquisition dates drawn on the charts so you can judge for yourself.

All figures independently sourced; every number is footnoted. Where the data doesn't exist, we say so instead of interpolating.

The part inflation can't explain

Sport by sport

Each chart shows average annual family spending per child in that sport, from parent surveys — nominal (light) and in constant 2025 dollars (dark). If the dark line is flat, inflation explains the increase. Vertical ticks mark private-equity and ownership events in that sport. Badges rate how much data actually exists — a lot of the loudest claims rest on the thinnest series.

Same scale makes cross-sport comparison honest; per-sport scale shows each trend's shape.
